Citadel of Qaitbay

A timeless guardian of the Mediterranean, where history stands proudly on the edge of the sea.”

The Citadel of Qaitbay is one of the most iconic defensive strongholds on the Mediterranean coast. Built in the 15th century by Sultan Al-Ashraf Qaitbay, it stands on the exact site of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Overlooking the sea with its massive stone walls and towers, the citadel was designed to protect Egypt’s northern borders from naval invasions. Today, it is not only a historical fortress but also a breathtaking landmark that blends military architecture with stunning coastal views. Visitors can walk through its corridors, explore its chambers, and enjoy panoramic scenes of the Mediterranean Sea, especially at sunset when the sky meets the waves in golden harmony
