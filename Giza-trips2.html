The Sphinx

A Silent guardian that has watched over Egypt for more than 4000 years.

The Great Sphinx of Giza is one of the most iconic and mysterious monuments in the world.
With the body of a powerful lion and the head of a human, it is widely believed to represent Pharaoh Khafre, symbolizing strength, wisdom, and royal authority.
Carved directly from a single massive limestone rock over 4,500 years ago, the Sphinx stands on the Giza Plateau, watching over the pyramids and the surrounding desert. Its enormous size and detailed craftsmanship reflect the incredible skill of ancient Egyptian builders. Throughout history, the Sphinx has been buried under sand and later rediscovered, adding to its mystery. The missing nose remains one of its most famous secrets, sparking many theories and stories. Today, the Sphinx continues to captivate visitors from around the world as a timeless symbol of ancient Egypt’s greatness and enduring legacy.
