Ras Mohammed National Park

A sanctuary where coral kingdoms bloom beneath turquoise waters and silence speaks louder than words.

Ras Mohammed National Park is one of the most stunning natural reserves in Egypt and the entire world. Located at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, where the Red Sea meets the Gulf of Aqaba, it is a paradise of crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and rich marine life. The park is home to more than a thousand species of fish, colorful corals, and rare sea creatures, making it one of the top diving and snorkeling destinations globally. Beyond the underwater beauty, Ras Mohammed also features dramatic cliffs, salt lakes, and desert landscapes that create a unique contrast between land and sea. It is not just a tourist destination—it is a protected natural wonder that showcases the raw, untouched beauty of Egypt’s environment.
